About Duckweed

Duckweed is often used in wastewater treatment due to its ability to absorb nutrients and pollutants from the water. It effectively removes excess nitrogen, phosphorus, heavy metals, and organic pollutants.

Nutrient Uptake: Duckweed absorbs high levels of nitrogen and phosphorus, which are common pollutants in agricultural runoff and wastewater. These nutrients can cause eutrophication, leading to algal blooms and oxygen depletion in water bodies. By removing these nutrients, duckweed helps prevent these adverse effects.


Heavy Metal Absorption: Duckweed can uptake and accumulate heavy metals such as cadmium, lead, mercury, and arsenic from contaminated water. This ability makes it useful in phytoremediation strategies for cleaning polluted environments. Organic Pollutants: It can break down organic pollutants and pesticides, further purifying water bodies.

How Duckweed Helps Clean Rivers and Other Water Bodies

Duckweed's role in cleaning rivers and other water bodies involves several mechanisms that contribute to improved water quality and ecosystem health.

  • 1. Nutrient Removal

    • Duckweed effectively removes excess nutrients from water bodies, which is crucial for preventing eutrophication.
    Nitrogen and Phosphorus Uptake: Duckweed absorbs dissolved nitrogen and phosphorus compounds, preventing nutrient overloads that lead to algal blooms and oxygen depletion. For example, in wastewater treatment, duckweed can reduce nitrogen and phosphorus concentrations by 80-90%

  • 2. Heavy Metal and Pollutant Absorption

    • Duckweed's ability to absorb and accumulate heavy metals makes it valuable in remediating polluted waters.
    Heavy Metal Phytoremediation: Duckweed species like Lemna minor have demonstrated the capacity to absorb metals such as cadmium, lead, and mercury, making them effective in cleaning industrial effluents and mining-affected waters.

  • 3. Algal Bloom Control

    • By covering the water's surface, duckweed reduces light penetration, limiting the growth of harmful algae.
    Shade Effect: Duckweed mats provide shade, reducing photosynthesis in algae and preventing algal blooms that deplete oxygen and produce toxins harmful to aquatic life.
