Clean Ganga Project Duckweed Deployment for River Restoration

  • Date: January 15-16, 2024
  • Location: Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Organizers: National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG) and Banaras Hindu University
  • Overview: This event was part of the larger Namami Gange program, focusing specifically on the role of duckweed in cleaning the Ganges River. Researchers and environmentalists gathered to discuss the potential of duckweed as a natural purifier, capable of reducing nutrient pollution and heavy metals in one of India’s most sacred rivers.
  • Outcome: The project successfully demonstrated the use of duckweed in select areas along the Ganga, showing significant reductions in nitrogen and phosphorus levels. The event included hands-on workshops for local communities, emphasizing the importance of sustainable practices and involving them in continuous monitoring.

Mithi River Revival DUCKWEED AS A SUSTAINABLE SOLUTION

  • Date: February 10, 2024
  • Location: Mumbai, Maharashtra, India
  • Organizers: Maharashtra Pollution Control Board (MPCB) and Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IITB)
  • Overview: The Mithi River project focused on leveraging duckweed’s phytoremediation capabilities to tackle pollution in urban waterways. Mumbai’s Mithi River, a vital waterway suffering from industrial pollution and sewage discharge, became a pilot site for duckweed deployment.
  • Outcome: The initiative led to a 25% reduction in biochemical oxygen demand (BOD) and a 30% decrease in ammonium concentrations. The success of this project has sparked interest in scaling similar solutions to other urban rivers across India.

Yamuna River Cleanup HARNESSING DUCKWEED FOR A HEALTHIER ECOSYSTEM

  • Date: March 22, 2024 (World Water Day)
  • Location: New Delhi, India
  • Organizers: Yamuna Action Plan (YAP) and Centre for Science and Environment (CSE)
  • Organizers: Yamuna Action Plan (YAP) and Centre for Science and Environment (CSE) Overview: On World Water Day, this event focused on using duckweed as a natural remedy for pollution in the Yamuna River, especially in the heavily polluted sections near Delhi. The project aimed to demonstrate duckweed's efficiency in absorbing excess nutrients and metals from industrial discharge.
  • Outcome: Over a three-month period, the project reported a 35% decrease in phosphorus and a 20% reduction in nitrate levels. Public awareness campaigns accompanied the event, educating residents about sustainable water management practices.
